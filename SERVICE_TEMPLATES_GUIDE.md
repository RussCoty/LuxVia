# Funeral Service Templates Guide

## Overview

LuxVia now includes pre-built funeral service templates to help you quickly set up appropriate liturgies and ceremonies based on different traditions and denominations. These templates provide a structured starting point that you can customize to meet your specific needs.

## Available Templates

### 1. Catholic Requiem Mass
A traditional Catholic funeral Mass following the Order of Christian Funerals.

**Structure:**
- **Introductory Rites**: Entrance song, greeting, sprinkling with holy water, placing of the pall, opening prayer
- **Liturgy of the Word**: First reading, responsorial psalm, second reading (optional), gospel acclamation, gospel reading, homily, prayer of the faithful
- **Liturgy of the Eucharist**: Offertory hymn, eucharistic prayer, Holy Holy Holy, Lord's Prayer, communion hymn
- **Final Commendation**: Invitation to prayer, words of remembrance (optional), prayer of commendation, song of farewell, recessional hymn

**Best for:** Catholic funerals following traditional Roman Catholic liturgy

### 2. Protestant Funeral Service
A traditional Protestant funeral service with hymns, scripture readings, and prayers.

**Structure:**
- **Opening**: Opening hymn, scripture sentences, opening prayer
- **Scripture and Reflection**: Old Testament reading, hymn or psalm, New Testament reading, sermon
- **Remembrance**: Eulogy, special music (optional)
- **Closing**: Prayers of comfort, closing hymn, benediction

**Best for:** Protestant denominations including Methodist, Presbyterian, Baptist, Lutheran, and other mainline Protestant traditions

### 3. Secular Memorial Service
A non-religious memorial service celebrating the life of the deceased.

**Structure:**
- **Welcome**: Opening music, welcome and introduction
- **Celebration of Life**: Reading or poem, life story, musical reflection, sharing of memories (optional)
- **Tribute**: Eulogy, reading or poem, special music (optional)
- **Closing**: Closing words, closing music, reception information (optional)

**Best for:** Non-religious or humanist ceremonies, interfaith services, or celebrations of life

## How to Use Templates

### Accessing Templates
1. Open the **Service** tab in LuxVia
2. Tap the **document icon** (ðŸ“„) in the top right corner
3. Browse available templates

### Applying a Template
1. Select a template from the list
2. Preview the template structure to see what sections and items it includes
3. Tap "Apply" to add the template to your service
4. Choose whether to:
   - **Replace** your current service (clears existing items)
   - **Add to Existing** (appends template items to your current service)

### Customizing Templates
After applying a template, you can:

- **Edit items**: Tap any item to modify its content
- **Reorder items**: Enter edit mode and drag items to reorder
- **Delete items**: Remove items marked as "(Optional)" or any items you don't need
- **Add new items**: Add additional songs, readings, or custom content
- **Replace placeholder text**: Update generic descriptions with specific songs, readings, or personal content

## Creating Custom Templates

While LuxVia provides several built-in templates, you can effectively create your own by:

1. Starting with the closest matching template
2. Customizing it to your needs
3. Saving it as your service order

For recurring services or family traditions, you can maintain consistency by:
- Documenting your customized service structure
- Using the same modifications for similar services
- Sharing your customized order with family members or clergy

## Tips for Using Templates

### Catholic Requiem Mass
- Consult with the parish priest about music selections and optional elements
- Verify that chosen hymns are approved for liturgical use
- Coordinate with the music director for responsorial psalm and other sung parts
- Some elements (like the placing of the pall) may be optional depending on parish customs

### Protestant Service
- Confirm hymn selections with the church or funeral home
- Coordinate with the minister about the sermon and any additional prayers
- Consider the deceased's favorite hymns or meaningful songs
- Verify the order of service with your pastor, as some denominations have specific preferences

### Secular Memorial
- Choose readings and music that reflect the deceased's values and personality
- Consider opening the floor for attendees to share memories
- Select a meaningful location that was significant to the deceased
- Keep the tone appropriate - celebratory yet respectful

## Common Elements

All templates include placeholders for:
- **Music/Songs**: Hymns, contemporary songs, or instrumental pieces
- **Readings**: Scripture passages, poems, or literary works
- **Personal tributes**: Eulogies and remembrances
- **Prayers or reflections**: Faith-based or secular meditations

## Adapting Templates for Other Traditions

The provided templates can be adapted for other traditions:

### Jewish Funerals
Start with the Secular template and add:
- Traditional prayers (Kaddish, El Malei Rachamim)
- Torah readings
- Psalms
- Hesped (eulogy)

### Muslim Funerals (Janazah)
Start with the Secular template and add:
- Quran recitation
- Salat al-Janazah (funeral prayer)
- Dua (supplications)

### Other Traditions
Use the Secular template as a base and incorporate:
- Traditional rituals specific to your culture or faith
- Appropriate music and readings
- Cultural customs and practices
